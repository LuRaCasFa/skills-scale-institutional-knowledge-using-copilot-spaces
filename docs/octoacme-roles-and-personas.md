# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Core Delivery Roles

### Developers

#### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

#### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

#### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

#### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

#### Interactions
- Works with Product Managers on acceptance criteria and prioritization
- Collaborates with UX Designers on implementation details
- Partners with DevOps/Release Engineers on deployment and CI/CD
- Receives feedback from QA and Support teams

---

### Product Managers

#### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

#### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics
- Work with Analytics leads to establish KPIs and measure impact

#### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

#### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs
- Monthly reviews of success metrics with Analytics

#### Interactions
- Collaborates with Project Managers on timelines and scope
- Works with UX Designers/Researchers on user needs and validation
- Partners with Developers on technical feasibility
- Engages Support/Customer Success for customer feedback
- Reviews analytics dashboards with Analytics leads

---

### Project Managers

#### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

#### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication
- Track delivery metrics and team velocity

#### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

#### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation
- Escalation and issue tracking

#### Interactions
- Works closely with Product Managers on scope and priorities
- Coordinates with Developers on capacity and scheduling
- Engages Scrum Master on process improvements
- Communicates with Support/Customer Success on go-live readiness
- Partners with DevOps on release scheduling

---

## Cross-Functional Support Roles

### Scrum Master / Agile Coach

#### Role Summary
The Scrum Master or Agile Coach facilitates effective delivery practices, removes process blockers, and coaches teams on Agile methodologies. This role ensures the team operates efficiently and continuously improves.

#### Responsibilities
- Facilitate daily standups, sprint planning, and retrospectives
- Remove impediments blocking team progress
- Coach team members on Agile principles and practices
- Track and help improve team velocity and process health
- Identify and escalate organizational blockers
- Support continuous improvement initiatives

#### Goals
- Enable high-performing, self-organizing teams
- Reduce cycle time and waste
- Foster psychological safety and engagement

#### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and team ceremonies
- 1-on-1 coaching and feedback
- Retrospective facilitation and action item tracking
- Metrics and team health dashboards

#### Interactions
- Works closely with Project Manager and Product Manager to resolve dependencies
- Supports Developers in removing technical blockers
- Collaborates with cross-functional team members on process optimization
- Participates in incident response to improve post-incident processes

---

### UX Designer / Researcher

#### Role Summary
UX Designers and Researchers define user journeys, validate design assumptions, and ensure solutions are usable and meet customer needs. This role bridges customer insights with product and technical implementation.

#### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability studies
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications
- Validate design decisions with users and stakeholders
- Collaborate with Product Managers to clarify user needs
- Work with Developers to ensure designs are technically feasible
- Participate in design reviews and iterate based on feedback

#### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, user-centered solutions
- Reduce design rework through early validation
- Improve customer satisfaction and adoption

#### Typical Communication
- Design sprints and workshops with stakeholders
- Usability test reports and findings
- Design specifications and component documentation
- Collaboration in sprint planning and acceptance criteria discussions

#### Interactions
- Partners with Product Managers to understand customer problems
- Works with Developers on technical feasibility and implementation
- Engages Support/Customer Success for post-launch feedback
- Collaborates with Analytics on measuring user experience success

---

### DevOps / Release Engineer

#### Role Summary
DevOps and Release Engineers automate and manage deployment pipelines, infrastructure, and system observability. This role ensures reliable, secure, and efficient software delivery.

#### Responsibilities
- Build and maintain CI/CD pipelines
- Automate infrastructure provisioning and deployment
- Monitor system health, performance, and security
- Manage release packaging and deployment scheduling
- Coordinate rollbacks and incident response for production issues
- Optimize build times and deployment efficiency

#### Goals
- Enable fast, reliable deployments with minimal risk
- Maintain high system uptime and performance
- Reduce time-to-recovery (MTTR) for incidents

#### Typical Communication
- Release notes and deployment plans
- Infrastructure and system monitoring dashboards
- Incident response and post-mortems
- CI/CD improvement proposals and documentation

#### Interactions
- Supports Developers in troubleshooting deployment issues
- Coordinates with Project Manager on release scheduling
- Works with Support/Customer Success on go-live and incident response
- Partners with Product Manager on feature flag management
- Collaborates with Analytics on telemetry and monitoring setup

---

### Support / Customer Success

#### Role Summary
Support and Customer Success teams serve as the voice of the customer, handling inquiries, flagging critical issues, and ensuring customer satisfaction. This role provides valuable feedback for product and engineering teams.

#### Responsibilities
- Provide timely customer support and issue resolution
- Triage and escalate critical bugs and feature requests
- Gather and communicate customer feedback and needs
- Track customer satisfaction metrics and trends
- Prepare for go-live activities and customer communication
- Participate in incident response and customer communication during outages

#### Goals
- Maximize customer satisfaction and retention
- Quickly identify and escalate product issues
- Convert customer feedback into actionable insights

#### Typical Communication
- Customer support tickets and issue tracking
- Weekly customer feedback summaries
- Pre-release coordination and go-live checklists
- Customer communication during incidents

#### Interactions
- Reports bugs and feature requests to Product Managers and Developers
- Participates in sprint planning to understand upcoming changes
- Works with Project Manager on release readiness and communication
- Engages with Analytics to track customer health and satisfaction metrics
- Coordinates with DevOps on incident communication

---

### Analytics / Data Lead

#### Role Summary
Analytics and Data Leads define measurement strategies, deliver insights, and help teams understand the impact of their work through data. This role enables data-driven decision making across the organization.

#### Responsibilities
- Define success metrics and KPIs aligned with business goals
- Implement instrumentation and data collection
- Deliver regular analytics dashboards and reports
- Conduct analysis to support A/B testing and experimentation
- Identify trends, anomalies, and optimization opportunities
- Advise on data privacy and compliance requirements

#### Goals
- Enable data-driven product and business decisions
- Quickly measure and iterate on product changes
- Identify and reduce friction in user journeys

#### Typical Communication
- Weekly and monthly analytics dashboards
- Insights and recommendations reports
- Data dictionaries and instrumentation specifications
- Experimentation results and learnings

#### Interactions
- Advises Product Managers on success metrics and measurement
- Partners with Developers on event tracking implementation
- Works with UX Designers on user experience metrics
- Collaborates with DevOps on system performance monitoring
- Supports Project Managers with delivery metrics and forecasting

---

## How These Personas Are Used

- Use these persona definitions to frame scenarios and sample interactions in exercises and project contexts
- Each persona can be used as a perspective for understanding process requirements and handoff points
- When documenting new processes, consider which personas are involved and what information they need to succeed
- Use the interaction maps to identify potential communication gaps or areas for process improvement

---

## Adding New Personas

As OctoAcme evolves, new personas may be needed. When adding a new role, ensure:
- Clear responsibilities and goals are defined
- Interaction points with existing personas are documented
- The persona aligns with OctoAcme's principles (customer-first, iterative, data-informed)
- The addition is reviewed and approved by core team leads
